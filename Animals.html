Animals

Sustainable eggs, dairy, & meat

No homestead is complete without raising animals!

A self-sufficient homestead needs animals for protein and fats. Whether you raise animals for a humane meat source, or just fresh eggs, there are a lot of things to consider.

  1. Eggs
    • How many eggs do you need each year? Some species are more prolific egg-layers than others.
    • How will you collect the eggs and clean up after the birds so they stay healthy?
    • What species of bird do you want to raise? Ducks, chickens, turkeys, and other birds have different needs for space, feed, water, and even shelter. Will you have the space they require?
    • Do you have a water source nearby? Ducks need daily access to water so they can clean their noses and stay healthy.
    • Do you have a chicken run? Chickens will dig up the roots of plants in search of bugs, so make sure to keep them away from your garden and rotate their run so they don't create a barren spot.
  2. Dairy
    • How much dairy do you need? Some mammalian species lactate more than others.
    • Which animals do you want to raise in order to get milk? Remember that the animal will only lactate after giving birth, and the baby will need to feed and be cared for.
    • Do you plan to make cheese? Do you prefer goat cheese or cheese made from cow's milk?
    • Will you have the time to milk your lactating animal every day to continue milk production and relieve discomfort?
  3. Meat
    • Will you be raising animals for meat specifically, or will you only butcher retired animals? Some species of chickens and ducks are both good egg-layers and a good size for meat.
    • What kind of meat do you want to eat? Larger animals like cows require plenty of space to graze and may require a professional butcher to handle the size and weight.
    • What diet will you provide for your animals so their meat is healthy and nutritous?
    • How will you insure your animals are butchered quickly and painlessly with minimal stress to the animal?
