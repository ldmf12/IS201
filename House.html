House

The beginning of your homestead

Your home is the foundation of your homesteading life. Where you decide to put down roots is not a decision you should make lightly.

There are three things to consider when choosing a home:

  1. Location
    • Is the climate amenable to you and your family?
    • Are you within an acceptable distance to schools, shops, and a hospital?
    • Do you like the community? Are you a good distance from your neighbors?
    • Does your property meet the requirements you've set for soil composition, water, space? If not, would you be able to make changes to meet those needs?
  2. Design
    • Which direction is your home facing? Will you get enough sunlight during times when you want it?
    • Does your house have the space for your family's needs, or can you renovate?
    • Does the look of your house appeal to you? Do you like the stairs or would you prefer a porch?
  3. Materials
    • What materials is your house made from? Does it trap heat in the winter and remain cool in the summer?
    • Is your home made from durable materials like stone, or will it need regular repairs like untreated wood?
    • What materials are you able to afford? Will it be more cost-effective for you to replace cheaper material every few years or to use more expensive materials now?

Renovating a house requires a lot of planning and can quickly become an exhausting and expensive endeavor. Making decisions now about what you want your homestead to look like will allow you to make changes early and avoid costly mistakes. If you want to grow old in your dream house, then consider a ranch design to avoid climbing stairs. If you want to grow your own herbs for cooking, consider making a greenhouse attached to the kitchen door. You can install vents that allow the heat from kitchen ovens to keep your plants warm in winter. See that the greenhouse faces the sun to get full advantage of the warmth, and consider a high stone base to hold heat throughout the night.
