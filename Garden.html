Garden

If you eat it, then why not grow it?

I've long held a goal to grow all the produce my family and I eat. Carrots and onions are one thing, but apples and oranges require time and planning.

Most of us won't be able to grow EVERYTHING we consume. Some items, like lentils, require machinery to harvest with any efficiency, and their ready availablilty in stores may be reason enough to simply stock up your pantry and call it a day. Other items, like raspberries, are so expensive in stores and easy to grow, that it may feel worth braving the thorns to enjoy the sweet treat. Whatever your gardening goals, a well-thought out plan will transform your experience.

The first thing to determine is the climate zone in which you live. Plants grow in specific conditions, and if your climate is not conducive to that plant's growth, then you will need to consider building a greenhouse or cold cellar to help your plants thrive. If you already know your zone, then feel free to skip to the next section!

Once you know the zone you live in, you can begin to plan which plants to cultivate in your yard, and that necesitates having space for them to grow.

Each plant is unique and has different requirements for sunlight, precipitation, soil composition, and space. Sunlight, preciptation, and soil can be altered artificially, but poor planning in the spacing of your plants is very difficult to correct. If you fail to give your plants enough room to grow, they will choke each other and have stunted growth. Before you begin, plan out how you will space your plants. Do your research! Some plants require only a few inches, while others need a couple of feet of space.

Once you know what you can plant in your area and where you will plant it, you can begin to experiment with greenhouses and cold cellars to cultivate plants which require warm or cool weather not naturally occuring in your area. This can vastly extend the species you can grow, and greenhouses can enable year-round production of favored summertime swets like strawberries!
